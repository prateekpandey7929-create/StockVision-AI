How the AI Model Works

Our platform utilizes yfinance to fetch active stock records. We engineer features to give our machine learning models predictive capability.

1
Data Collection & Engineering

We compute 5, 10, and 20-day Simple Moving Averages, historical closing price lags, spreads, and rolling volatilities.

2
Chronological Backtest Splitting

We split data sequentially (80% train, 20% test) to prevent data leakage and evaluate the model's performance on unseen chronological events.

3
Ensemble Regressor Fitting

A Random Forest Regressor fits decision trees on bagging subsets to output the final expected close, scoring R² accuracy.

Engineered ML Features

  • Open, High, Low, Close, Volume: Raw current indicators.
  • Close Lags (t-1, t-2, t-3): Captures short-term daily momentum.
  • SMAs (5, 10, 20 days): Calculates rolling support trends.
  • High-Low Price Spread: Tracks daily intraday range.
  • Volatility (5 days): Measures recent risk and spread fluctuations.

Frequently Asked Questions

Clear details regarding yfinance operations and machine learning limitations.

What machine learning model is utilized for stock prediction?
StockVision AI primarily uses a Random Forest Regressor, an ensemble bagging algorithm that aggregates results from 150 decision trees to forecast closing prices. We also train a Linear Regression baseline to provide a benchmark comparison for the R² score, MAE, and RMSE metrics.
Where does the stock data come from?
All data is fetched in real-time from the Yahoo Finance API (yfinance). When you search for a stock or run a prediction, the app pulls historical trading session logs (Open, High, Low, Close, Volume) covering the last 2 years.
Can I search for international stocks?
Yes! Any ticker supported by Yahoo Finance can be analyzed. This includes US stocks (e.g. AAPL, TSLA), Indian indices/shares (e.g. RELIANCE.NS, TCS.NS, ^NSEI), cryptocurrencies, and other international tickers.
Is this financial advice?
Absolutely not. StockVision AI is an educational tool demonstrating technical indicator integration with scikit-learn models. Financial markets are highly complex and influenced by sentiment, news, macroeconomics, and unpredictable volatility. Never execute real trades based solely on models.
